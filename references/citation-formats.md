# Citation Formats

Quick reference for formatting citations in the Full Source List.

---

## APA (default — use unless the brief specifies otherwise)

**Journal article:**
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. _Journal Name_, _Volume_(Issue), page–page. https://doi.org/xxxxx

**Book:**
Author, A. A. (Year). _Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle_. Publisher.

**Government/institutional report:**
Organisation Name. (Year). _Title of report_. Publisher. URL

**Webpage:**
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). _Title of page_. Website Name. URL

**No author:**
_Title of page or article_. (Year). Website Name. URL

---

## MLA (use only if specified in brief)

**Journal article:**
Author Last, First. "Article Title." _Journal Name_, vol. X, no. Y, Year, pp. X–X.

**Webpage:**
Author Last, First. "Page Title." _Website Name_, Day Month Year, URL.

---

## Chicago (use only if specified in brief)

**Journal article (footnote):**
First Last, "Article Title," _Journal Name_ Volume, no. Issue (Year): page–page.

**Report:**
Organisation. _Report Title_. City: Publisher, Year.

---

## In-text citation (APA inline format used in Findings Template)

Use this format immediately after each finding:
(Source: Author/Organisation, _Title abbreviated_, Year, URL)

Example:
"Antibiotic resistance rates in Southeast Asia increased 32% between 2015 and 2022."
(Source: WHO, _Global Antimicrobial Resistance and Use Surveillance System Report_, 2023, https://who.int/glass)

---

## When no author is listed

Use the organisation name in place of the author. If the source has no clear author
or organisation, it is likely not Tier 1–3 and should be reconsidered.

---

## DOIs and URLs

- Always include a DOI if one exists (preferred over URL — DOIs are stable).
- For URLs, use the direct link to the document, not a search results page.
- Do not use URL shorteners.
- Confirm the URL resolves before citing it.
