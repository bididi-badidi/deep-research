FINDINGS — personal-computing-ai
====================
Sub-question answered : Investigate the 'More Personal Computing' (MPC) segment from April 2023 to March 2026, covering Windows OEM, Surface/Hardware, Gaming (Activision Blizzard impact), and the strategic shift toward AI PCs/Copilot+.
Category              : Evidence / Applied / Emerging
Confidence level      : High
Confidence rationale  : Findings are based on primary data from Microsoft Investor Relations (FY23-FY26 Q2), SEC filings, and reputable financial/tech analysis (Thurrott, Windows Central, Bloomberg). Note that a structural reporting change in FY2025 affects direct YoY comparisons for the Windows segment.

KEY FINDINGS
------------
1. **Total Segment Volatility and Growth:** The More Personal Computing (MPC) segment revenue fluctuated significantly, from a low of ~$13.2B in early FY2023 (post-pandemic slump) to peaks exceeding $16.9B in FY2024, driven by the $69 billion Activision Blizzard acquisition and the emergence of AI-integrated hardware. (Source: Microsoft, *Earnings Press Releases FY23-FY26*, 2023-2026)

2. **Gaming & Activision Blizzard Impact:** The Activision Blizzard acquisition closed in October 2023 (Q2 FY2024). It immediately added ~15-50 points of growth to total Gaming revenue. In Q2 FY24, Gaming revenue grew 49% YoY (44 points from Activision), and by Q1 FY25, it was the primary driver of MPC growth. (Source: Microsoft, *FY24 Q2 & FY25 Q1 Performance Metrics*, 2024-2025)

3. **Windows OEM Recovery & AI PCs:** After double-digit declines in FY2023 due to high channel inventory, Windows OEM revenue stabilized in FY2024 and grew significantly in FY2025 and early FY2026 (+18% in Q1 FY26). This recovery was fueled by two factors: the Windows 10 end-of-support (October 2025) upgrade cycle and the introduction of the "AI PC" category featuring dedicated NPUs. (Source: Microsoft, *FY26 Q1 Earnings Call Transcript*, Oct 2025; Thurrott, *Microsoft Earnings Analysis FY25*, 2025)

4. **Surface & Hardware Pivot:** Surface revenue declined for seven consecutive quarters through mid-2024 (down 24% in FY23, 9-11% in FY24). However, FY2025 saw a recovery to $8.9 billion in revenue, driven by the launch of Copilot+ PCs (Surface Pro 11, Surface Laptop 7) which targeted enterprise AI adoption and increased Average Selling Price (ASP) by ~$112. (Source: Windows Central, *Surface Revenue and Copilot+ Launch*, 2024; sqmagazine.co.uk, *Surface Revenue Milestone*, 2025)

5. **Copilot+ Integration Strategy:** Microsoft transitioned Windows from a traditional OS to an "AI-first" platform starting in mid-2024. The Copilot+ PC requirement (40+ TOPS NPU) became the new baseline for premium Windows hardware. This strategic shift successfully pivoted the segment from a stagnant consumer market toward a high-margin enterprise "AI-ready" hardware ecosystem. (Source: Microsoft, *Build 2024 Keynote*, May 2024; PC World, *Copilot+ PC Market Impact*, 2025)

6. **2026 Supply Chain Headwinds:** By early 2026, the segment faced margin pressure from global memory (RAM) and SSD shortages caused by AI data center demand. Microsoft implemented price hikes of $50-$500 across the Surface line in April 2026 to preserve margins, while guiding for a revenue slowdown ($12.3B-$12.8B) as the Windows 10 refresh cycle peaked. (Source: VarIndia, *Global Memory Shortage and Surface Pricing*, April 2026; Barchart, *Microsoft FY26 Q3 Guidance*, April 2026)

CONFLICTING EVIDENCE
--------------------
- **FY24 Q2 Revenue Discrepancy:** Some analyst reports initially cited MPC revenue as $14.2B for Q2 FY24, missing the full-quarter inclusion of Activision Blizzard. SEC filings confirm the higher $16.9B figure.
- **Copilot+ Adoption Rates:** While Microsoft reported "record interest," some third-party analysts (e.g., IDC, early 2025) noted that consumer adoption lagged behind enterprise due to the "AI performance tax" (higher entry prices for NPU-equipped laptops). (Source: IDC, *Global PC Tracker 2025*)

SOURCE QUALITY NOTES
--------------------
- **Segment Restatement (Aug 2024):** Microsoft moved "Windows Commercial products and cloud services" out of MPC and into "Productivity and Business Processes" in FY25. This makes "Windows OEM" the primary metric for MPC Windows revenue from that point forward.
- **FY26 Q3/Q4 Data:** As of April 23, 2026, Q3 results are projected but not finalized (scheduled for April 29 release).
