2. **Top-Level Growth Drivers (MD&A)**

*   **Fiscal Year 2023:** Driven by "Microsoft Cloud" (revenue up 22% to $111.6B). Key factors included Azure consumption-based services, Office 365 Commercial seat growth, and expansion in average revenue per user (ARPU). Early infusion of generative AI across the tech stack was noted as a strategic shift. (Source: Microsoft, *2023 Form 10-K*, 2023)
*   **Fiscal Year 2024:** Driven by "Cloud and AI services" (Microsoft Cloud revenue up 23% to $135B). Azure and other cloud services grew 30% (33% constant currency). Growth also included the impact of the Activision Blizzard acquisition on gaming revenue and the scaling of AI innovation via Microsoft 365 Copilot and Azure AI infrastructure. (Source: Microsoft, *2024 Form 10-K*, 2024)
*   **Fiscal Year 2025:** Driven by the "AI platform shift." Azure revenue surpassed $75B (up 34%), significantly fueled by AI workloads. Microsoft Cloud revenue reached $168.9B (up 23%). Other contributors included Dynamics 365 (up 19%) and Search/Advertising (up 20%), alongside the emergence of "agentic" AI scenarios. (Source: Microsoft, *2025 Form 10-K*, 2025)
*   **Fiscal Year 2026 (YTD):** Growth continues to be led by AI infrastructure scaling and Azure consumption. Management has highlighted the integration of AI across all layers of the tech stack as the primary driver of operational leverage, despite volatility in net income due to equity investment valuations (OpenAI). (Source: Microsoft, *FY26 Q1 & Q2 10-Qs*, 2025-2026)
