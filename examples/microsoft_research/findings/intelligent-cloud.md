FINDINGS — INTELLIGENT-CLOUD-2026
====================
Sub-question answered : Analyse the 'Intelligent Cloud' segment performance from April 2023 to March 2026, tracking Azure revenue, AI adoption, infrastructure scaling, and partnership impacts.
Category              : Evidence / Comparative
Confidence level      : High
Confidence rationale  : Data is synthesized from official Microsoft quarterly earnings releases (FY23 Q4 – FY26 Q2), SEC filings, and documented AI infrastructure roadmaps. FY26 Q3 data includes official company guidance provided in January 2026.

KEY FINDINGS
------------
1. **Intelligent Cloud Segment Revenue:** The segment experienced significant growth, with quarterly revenue rising from $24.0B in FY23 Q4 to $32.9B in FY26 Q2. Note that FY24 figures were restated downward in August 2024 (e.g., FY24 Q4 from $28.5B to $23.8B) following the transfer of EMS and Power BI per-user revenue to the Productivity segment.
   - FY23 Q4: $24.0B (Original)
   - FY24 (Restated): Q1: $20.0B, Q2: $21.5B, Q3: $22.0B, Q4: $23.8B
   - FY25: Q1: $24.1B, Q2: $25.5B, Q3: $26.6B, Q4: $29.9B
   - FY26: Q1: $30.9B, Q2: $32.9B
   (Source: Microsoft Investor Relations, Earnings Performance Reports FY24-FY26)

2. **Azure Revenue Growth (Constant Currency):** Azure growth accelerated from 27% in mid-2023 to a peak of 40% in late 2025, driven primarily by generative AI demand.
   - FY23 Q4: 27%
   - FY24: Q1: 30% (Restated), Q2: 30% (Restated), Q3: 33% (Restated), Q4: 35% (Restated)
   - FY25: Q1: 34%, Q2: 31%, Q3: 35%, Q4: 38%
   - FY26: Q1: 39%, Q2: 39%, Q3 (Guidance): 37-38%
   (Source: Microsoft, FY25 Investor Metrics Restatement; FY26 Q2 Earnings Transcript)

3. **AI Services Adoption:** The contribution of AI services to Azure's constant currency growth increased from ~1 percentage point in FY24 Q1 to ~16 points by FY25 Q3. Azure OpenAI Service organizational customers grew from 11,000 in mid-2023 to over 230,000 by Q3 2025.
   (Source: Microsoft Annual Reports 2023-2024; About Chromebooks, Azure OpenAI Statistics 2026)

4. **AI Infrastructure Milestones:**
   - **Nov 2023:** Launch of custom silicon Azure Maia 100 (AI accelerator) and Azure Cobalt 100 (CPU).
   - **Mar 2024:** General availability of NC H100 v5 VMs (NVIDIA H100) and announcement of Blackwell (GB200) adoption.
   - **Dec 2024:** Delivery of first NVIDIA Blackwell-based servers (GB200) following yield-related delays.
   - **Jan 2026:** Introduction of Maia 200 (3nm) for large-scale model inference (e.g., GPT-5).
   (Source: Tech Monitor, Nov 2023; Tom's Hardware, Sep 2024; ITdaily, Jan 2026)

5. **OpenAI Partnership Impact:** OpenAI-related commitments reached 45% of Azure's $625B Remaining Performance Obligation (RPO) backlog by January 2026. Key model releases integrated into Azure include GPT-4 Turbo (Apr 2024), GPT-4o (May 2024), o1 reasoning series (Sep 2024), and GPT-5 (Aug 2025).
   (Source: Windows Central, Jan 2026; Data Science Collective, Aug 2025)

6. **Server Products and Cloud Services:** This sub-segment grew 21-24% CC in FY24, accelerating to 31% by FY26 Q2. Growth in "Server products" (on-premises) remained low single-digit or negative, highlighting the structural shift to Azure and hybrid Azure Arc solutions.
   (Source: Microsoft, FY24-FY26 Earnings Releases)

CONFLICTING EVIDENCE
--------------------
- **Blackwell Timeline:** Initial expectations for Q3 2024 shipments were pushed to December 2024 due to NVIDIA packaging yield issues, impacting Azure's capacity ramp in late 2024.
- **Reporting Structure:** The August 2024 reclassification makes direct comparison between "Original" FY23/FY24 figures and FY25/FY26 figures difficult without using the restated metrics provided in Exhibit 99.1 (Aug 21, 2024).
