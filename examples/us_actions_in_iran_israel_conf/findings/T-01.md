FINDINGS — T-01
====================
Sub-question answered : Research and detail official US military actions regarding the Iran-Israel conflict from April 2024 to the present.
Category              : Evidence
Confidence level      : High
Confidence rationale  : Findings are based on official US Department of Defense (DoD) and US Central Command (CENTCOM) statements and press releases.

KEY FINDINGS
------------
1. On April 13–14, 2024, US forces intercepted over 80 one-way attack unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) and at least six ballistic missiles launched from Iran and Yemen targeting Israel. US Central Command (CENTCOM) forces, supported by US European Command destroyers, conducted these defensive actions primarily using the USS Arleigh Burke, USS Carney, and F-15E Strike Eagle aircraft. (Source: US CENTCOM, "US Central Command Update on Iran’s Attack on Israel," April 14, 2024, https://www.centcom.mil/MEDIA/PRESS-RELEASES/Press-Release-View/Article/3740601/us-central-command-update-on-irans-attack-on-israel/)

2. On August 11, 2024, Secretary of Defense Lloyd Austin ordered the USS Abraham Lincoln Carrier Strike Group, equipped with F-35C fighters, to accelerate its transit to the Central Command area of responsibility to bolster regional defense. He also ordered the USS Georgia (SSGN 729), a guided-missile submarine, to the same region. (Source: US Department of Defense, "Statement from Secretary of Defense Lloyd J. Austin III on U.S. Forces in the Middle East," August 11, 2024, https://www.defense.gov/News/Releases/Release/Article/3869111/statement-from-secretary-of-defense-lloyd-j-austin-iii-on-us-forces-in-the-middl/)

3. On August 21, 2024, the USS Abraham Lincoln (CVN 72) and its carrier strike group arrived in the Middle East, establishing a dual-carrier presence in the region alongside the USS Theodore Roosevelt (CVN 71) to deter Iranian escalation following the assassination of Hamas leader Ismail Haniyeh in Tehran. (Source: US CENTCOM, "USS Abraham Lincoln Carrier Strike Group Arrives in the Middle East," August 21, 2024, https://www.centcom.mil/MEDIA/PRESS-RELEASES/Press-Release-View/Article/3880909/uss-abraham-lincoln-carrier-strike-group-arrives-in-the-u-s-central-command-area/)

4. On October 1, 2024, US Navy destroyers USS Bulkeley and USS Cole, stationed in the Eastern Mediterranean, fired approximately a dozen interceptors (including SM-3 missiles) to assist the Israeli Air Force in intercepting a barrage of nearly 180 Iranian ballistic missiles. (Source: US Department of Defense, "Pentagon Press Secretary Maj. Gen. Pat Ryder Holds a Press Briefing," October 1, 2024, https://www.defense.gov/News/Transcripts/Transcript/Article/3922896/pentagon-press-secretary-maj-gen-pat-ryder-holds-a-press-briefing/)

5. On October 13, 2024, the US Department of Defense announced the deployment of a Terminal High Altitude Area Defense (THAAD) battery and a crew of approximately 100 US military personnel to Israel. This deployment was intended to integrate with Israel's existing air defense system following the October 1 Iranian missile attack. (Source: US Department of Defense, "Secretary of Defense Directs the Deployment of a THAAD Battery to Israel," October 13, 2024, https://www.defense.gov/News/Releases/Release/Article/3934493/secretary-of-defense-directs-the-deployment-of-a-thaad-battery-to-israel/)

6. On November 1, 2024, the Pentagon announced the deployment of additional military assets to the Middle East, including B-52 Stratofortress long-range bombers, tanker aircraft, and additional Navy destroyers. These forces were ordered to the region as the USS Abraham Lincoln Carrier Strike Group prepared to depart, maintaining a significant US combat power presence for regional deterrence. (Source: US Department of Defense, "Statement by Pentagon Press Secretary Maj. Gen. Pat Ryder on U.S. Posture in the Middle East," November 1, 2024, https://www.defense.gov/News/Releases/Release/Article/3952936/statement-by-pentagon-press-secretary-maj-gen-pat-ryder-on-us-posture-in-the-mid/)

CONFLICTING EVIDENCE
--------------------
None found. Official DoD statements and CENTCOM reports are consistent regarding deployment dates and mission objectives.

SOURCE QUALITY NOTES
--------------------
The primary sources used are official US Department of Defense and US Central Command press releases and briefing transcripts. These are considered Tier 1 sources for documenting official military posture and actions. No significant concerns regarding accuracy or methodology.

COVERAGE GAPS
-------------
- Specific details on the exact locations of certain stealth assets (e.g., F-22 or B-2 aircraft) are often withheld for operational security, though their presence in the theater is officially confirmed.
- Detailed counts of missiles intercepted by specific platforms in the October 1 attack remain partially classified; general "approximate dozen" figures are reported by the Pentagon.

SUGGESTED FOLLOW-UP
-------------------
1. Monitor the status of the USS Abraham Lincoln's departure and any successor carrier strike group deployments to the Mediterranean or Arabian Seas.
2. Investigate the integration level and command structure of the US-manned THAAD battery within the Israeli Defense Forces (IDF) air defense network.
