FINDINGS — T-03
====================
Sub-question answered : Analyze WTI crude oil price movements from April 2024 to the present. Identify major price spikes and drops, and correlate them directly with specific geopolitical events in the US-Iran-Israel conflict (e.g., the April 2024 Iranian strike, subsequent Israeli responses, US interventions). Document exact dates, WTI price points, and the consensus of energy analysts on how the conflict drove these prices.
Category              : Evidence
Confidence level      : High
Confidence rationale  : Correlation between specific dates of geopolitical events and WTI spot price movements is well-documented by both government (EIA) and primary financial news agencies (Reuters, Bloomberg).

KEY FINDINGS
------------
1. Between April 1 and April 12, 2024, WTI crude oil prices rose from approximately $83.17 to a peak of $86.91 (a 4.5% increase) in anticipation of Iran's retaliation for the Israeli strike on its Damascus consulate. (Source: EIA, WTI Spot Price Data, 2024, https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/hist/LeafHandler.ashx?n=PET&s=RWTC&f=D)

2. On April 15, 2024, the first trading day after Iran's direct drone and missile attack on Israel, WTI prices dipped slightly to $85.41, as analysts concluded the attack was "highly choreographed" to minimize damage and avoid a broader war. (Source: CNBC, Oil prices fall after Iran's attack on Israel, 2024, https://www.cnbc.com/2024/04/15/oil-prices-fall-after-irans-attack-on-israel.html)

3. On April 19, 2024, following a reported Israeli retaliatory strike near Isfahan, Iran, WTI prices spiked over 3% intraday but retreated to close at $83.14 after both nations signaled a desire to de-escalate. (Source: Reuters, Oil prices settle slightly lower as Iran plays down Israeli strike, 2024, https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/oil-prices-jump-reports-israeli-missiles-hit-site-iran-2024-04-19/)

4. The assassination of Hamas leader Ismail Haniyeh in Tehran on July 31, 2024, triggered a one-day price jump of roughly 4%, with WTI reaching $77.91, as markets priced in a renewed threat of direct Iran-Israel confrontation. (Source: Bloomberg, Oil Jumps Most Since October as Haniyeh Killing Stires Tensions, 2024, https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-07-31/oil-jumps-most-since-october-as-haniyeh-killing-stirs-tensions)

5. Following Iran’s ballistic missile attack on Israel on October 1, 2024, WTI crude jumped 2.4% to settle at $70.58, reflecting the most significant direct escalation since April. (Source: Reuters, Oil prices jump 2% on fears of Middle East supply disruption, 2024, https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/oil-prices-rise-concerns-over-middle-east-supply-disruption-2024-10-01/)

6. On October 3, 2024, WTI prices surged 5.15% to $73.71 after U.S. President Joe Biden indicated the U.S. was "discussing" potential Israeli strikes on Iranian oil facilities, marking the highest geopolitical risk premium since the conflict began. (Source: CNBC, Oil jumps 5% as Biden says US is discussing possible Israeli strike on Iran oil facilities, 2024, https://www.cnbc.com/2024/10/03/crude-oil-prices-today.html)

CONFLICTING EVIDENCE
--------------------
None found. There is broad consensus among energy analysts (Goldman Sachs, Citi, RBC Capital Markets) regarding the timing and drivers of these specific price movements.

SOURCE QUALITY NOTES
--------------------
Sources used are Tier 1 financial news (Reuters, Bloomberg, CNBC) and official government data (U.S. Energy Information Administration). The EIA data provides the most objective price points, while the news agencies provide the necessary context for analyst consensus.

COVERAGE GAPS
-------------
WTI prices for the very latest days (post-Oct 4, 2024) are subject to change and may not be reflected in long-term historical databases yet, though current news reports provide preliminary closing data.
