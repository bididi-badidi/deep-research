FINDINGS — T-02
====================
Sub-question answered : What are the official US diplomatic and economic actions regarding the Iran-Israel conflict from April 2024 to the present?
Category              : Evidence
Confidence level      : High
Confidence rationale  : Information is drawn from official US Department of State and Department of the Treasury public announcements, as well as UN Security Council records.

KEY FINDINGS
------------
1. **April 18, 2024: Sanctions on Iran’s UAV Program.** Following Iran's April 13 direct attack on Israel, the US Treasury (OFAC) sanctioned 16 individuals and two entities that enable Iran’s UAV production, specifically those associated with the IRGC. (Source: US Department of the Treasury, "Treasury Sanctions Iran’s UAV Program," April 18, 2024, https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy2260)

2. **April 18, 2024: UN Security Council Veto on Palestinian Membership.** The US vetoed a resolution for full Palestinian UN membership, arguing that Palestinian statehood should result from direct negotiations between Israel and the PA, a move seen as maintaining diplomatic alignment with Israel during regional tensions with Iran. (Source: UN News, "US vetoes Security Council resolution on Palestinian UN membership," April 18, 2024, https://news.un.org/en/story/2024/04/1148732)

3. **April 25, 2024: Additional UAV/Missile Sanctions.** The US and UK jointly imposed further sanctions on over a dozen entities, individuals, and vessels involved in Iran's UAV and ballistic missile programs. (Source: US Department of State, "Imposing Further Sanctions on Iran," April 25, 2024, https://www.state.gov/imposing-further-sanctions-on-iran/)

4. **May 31, 2024: Three-Phase Ceasefire Proposal.** President Biden announced a comprehensive three-phase ceasefire proposal for Gaza, which US diplomats spent subsequent months pressuring both Israel and regional proxies to accept to avoid a wider regional war involving Iran. (Source: The White House, "Remarks by President Biden on the Middle East," May 31, 2024)

5. **June 10, 2024: Adoption of UNSC Resolution 2735.** The US-drafted resolution supporting the May 31 ceasefire proposal was adopted by the UN Security Council (14-0, Russia abstained). (Source: UN Security Council, "Resolution 2735 (2024)," June 10, 2024)

6. **June 25, 2024: Sanctions on "Shadow Banking" Network.** OFAC sanctioned nearly 50 entities and individuals constituting a "shadow banking" network used by Iran’s Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Logistics (MODAFL) and the IRGC. (Source: US Department of the Treasury, "Treasury Sanctions Shadow Banking Network," June 25, 2024)

7. **August 15, 2024: Doha Mediation Summit.** US officials, including CIA Director Bill Burns, participated in high-level mediation in Doha with Qatar and Egypt to finalize the "bridging proposal" for a ceasefire and hostage release. (Source: US Department of State, "Joint Statement from the United States, Egypt, and Qatar," August 16, 2024)

8. **September 25, 2024: 21-Day Ceasefire Proposal for Lebanon.** The US and France, with support from several allies, called for an immediate 21-day ceasefire across the Israel-Lebanon border to prevent a full-scale war between Israel and the Iranian-backed Hezbollah. (Source: The White House, "Joint Statement on Lebanon," September 25, 2024)

9. **October 11, 2024: Expansion of Petroleum Sanctions.** In response to Iran’s October 1 missile attack on Israel, the US expanded sanctions on Iran’s petroleum and petrochemical sectors, identifying the "Ghost Fleet" of tankers used to circumvent existing sanctions. (Source: US Department of the Treasury, "Treasury Expands Sanctions on Iran’s Petroleum and Petrochemical Sectors," October 11, 2024)

10. **October 13, 2024: THAAD Deployment and Diplomatic Coordination.** While a military action, the deployment of the THAAD battery was accompanied by high-level diplomatic coordination between Secretary Austin and Minister Gallant to manage the scope of Israel's retaliatory response to Iran. (Source: US Department of Defense, "Statement on the Deployment of a THAAD Battery to Israel," October 13, 2024)

11. **October 15, 2024: Diplomatic Pressure on Humanitarian Aid.** Secretary Blinken and Secretary Austin sent a formal letter to Israeli officials demanding "urgent and sustained" actions to improve the humanitarian situation in Gaza within 30 days, linking future US military assistance to compliance. (Source: US Department of State, "Press Briefing," October 15, 2024)

12. **November 2024: Active Mediation for Israel-Lebanon Ceasefire.** US Special Envoy Amos Hochstein conducted multiple rounds of diplomacy in Beirut and Jerusalem to finalize a ceasefire framework between Israel and Hezbollah, aimed at decoupling the Lebanon and Gaza fronts. (Source: US Department of State, "Department Press Briefing," November 2024)

CONFLICTING EVIDENCE
--------------------
None found. US official actions are consistently documented in Treasury and State Department archives.

SOURCE QUALITY NOTES
--------------------
Sources are primary official government communications (Treasury, State Dept, White House). They provide high-fidelity dates and specific entity names.

COVERAGE GAPS
-------------
- Specific details of non-public "back-channel" diplomatic messages sent via the Swiss Embassy in Tehran are not included as they are not "official actions" in the public record.
- Full text of the October 15 Blinken-Austin letter was leaked but not officially published as a stand-alone document by the State Department, though its contents were confirmed in briefings.

SUGGESTED FOLLOW-UP
-------------------
1. Monitor the December 2024 implementation of the Lebanon ceasefire (if finalized) to see US monitoring mechanisms.
2. Investigate the economic impact of the "Ghost Fleet" sanctions on Iranian oil exports in Q4 2024.
